Hydra Audio Bridge

Eight bridges. Any app.

Eight fixed audio devices — from 2 to 128 channels — that any app can pick as its input or output, just like a real interface. Turn the ones you need on; leave the rest off.

The grid

Patch like a pro.

A Dante Controller–style matrix. Click a cross‑point to connect a transmitter to a receiver. Channel strips give you gain and VST3 inserts; a pin lights when audio is flowing.

Flux · Capture flows

Capture and route, continuously.

A new view, alongside Grid and List. A flow taps a device’s output — whatever any app is playing to it — and routes it into Hydra, without changing your system output. Each flow is a signal chain you edit in place: capture, then send wherever you want, with the same inserts and level as everywhere else.

Per‑app capture

Capture any app.

Grab the sound of a single app with Core Audio process taps. It keeps playing through its normal output while Hydra gets a clean copy you can route, record or stream.

VST3 channel strips

Plug‑ins in the signal path.

Insert VST3 plug‑ins on any channel, with live editor windows. By default each strip runs crash‑isolated, so a misbehaving plug‑in can never take the audio down. Plug‑ins load only when something is patched through them.

Audio over IP

Across the network.

AES67 (including Dante® devices in AES67 mode), NDI® and OSC — send and receive audio and control over the wire. Flag any bridge to transmit, and it appears on the network.

Built for the Mac

One app. Five languages.

Hydra runs as a single process — one icon in the Dock, no background daemon. It lives in the menu bar, follows your Mac’s accent colour, and speaks English, Português, Español, Français, Deutsch and Italiano.
